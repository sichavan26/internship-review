02 — Accomplishments & outcomes

What did I accomplish, and why it mattered

Outcomes at a glance

2
Production apps migrated to RAT pipelines — Cloudoscope and EGW cut over from legacy platform-pipelines to Release Automation. Why it mattered: The old pipeline was being deprecated. Without this migration, both apps would have lost their deployment path, blocking all future releases.
6→3
Snowflake finance views consolidated — Reduced 6 per-hyperscaler views to 3. Why it mattered: Finance stakeholders were confused by duplicated public/private views and inconsistent data. The consolidation gave them a single source of truth per hyperscaler and unblocked Sales Ops reporting for GCP and Azure.
8+
Strict AC met on Kafka consumer — Delivered smoke check consumer with zero existing code changed and real broker tests. Why it mattered: The team was migrating from UMB to Kafka. This consumer was the first step in decommissioning UMB dependencies, enabling the broader messaging migration.
5
Provider test classes eliminated — Refactored 5 copy-pasted abstract classes into one generic AbstractEntityKafkaPublishingITest. Why it mattered: Every new provider required duplicating 200+ lines of test setup. Now adding a new provider test is a single class with zero boilerplate.
1
New AWS API capability shipped — licenseArn support in ResolveCustomer API, deployed to production. Why it mattered: AWS is mandating concurrent agreements and licenseArn for all listings after 2026-06-01. Without this, Red Hat would lose the ability to onboard new AWS Marketplace customers.
